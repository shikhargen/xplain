How a post earns reach

A post does not simply "go viral." It must be retrieved, survive filters, predict valuable viewer actions, avoid negative feedback, and compete inside a diverse feed.

From scratch

The feed is a competition for each viewer

For every viewer, the system starts with their recent action history: posts they liked, replied to, reposted, dwelled on, ignored, blocked, muted, or reported. That history becomes the viewer context. Candidate posts are then pulled from followed accounts, global retrieval models, topic retrieval, cached candidates, and other sources.

1

Feed
  1. Query hydration. Fetch viewer action sequence, follows, mutes, blocks, topics, served history, IP and context.
  2. Sources. Pull in-network posts from followed accounts and out-of-network candidates from Phoenix retrieval, topics, MoE, and TweetMixer.
  3. Candidate hydration. Add post text, author info, media, language, quote data, video duration, subscription state, and safety labels.
  4. Pre-score filters. Remove duplicates, old posts, self posts, retweet duplicates, muted authors, muted keywords, seen posts, served posts, video exclusions, and topic mismatches.
  5. Phoenix prediction. A transformer predicts action probabilities for each candidate in the viewer context.
  6. Final ranking. Combine weighted probabilities, normalize, apply author diversity, downweight some out-of-network cases, and optionally run a value model ranker.
  7. Post-selection. Apply visibility filtering, ancillary filtering, conversation deduplication, then blend posts with ads and modules.

Non-obvious levers

What strong accounts optimize for

A

Author embedding

Your account identity enters retrieval. A coherent lane makes the author easier to match to users who already engage with that lane.

D

Dwell, not just clicks

The model predicts dwell and not-dwelled outcomes. Thin hooks that cause fast exits can underperform deeper posts.

F

Follow intent

Follow-author probability is a ranking input. Posts that make the account feel worth following can have extra value.

N

Negative risk

Not interested, mute, block, report, and not-dwelled are direct score inputs or filters. Avoid bait that polarizes outside the intended audience.

R

Repeat penalty

Same-author diversity means flooding can cannibalize distribution. Fewer high-conviction posts can beat volume bursts.

Q

Quote mechanics

Quote, quoted click, and quoted video view are separate predictions. Good framing can turn another post into a high-signal candidate.

Model intuition

Two models, two jobs

Retrieval model

Compress the viewer into a vector. Compress posts plus authors into vectors. Pull the nearest candidates from a huge corpus.

Ranking model

Place user, history, and candidates into a transformer. Candidates attend to user and history, not to one another, so each score is stable.

Practical synthesis

Account and post playbook

For every post

  • Lead with a clear promise so the right viewers keep reading.
  • Create reply-worthy specificity, not vague engagement bait.
  • Make reposting socially useful: frameworks, charts, checklists, proof.
  • Use media when it increases comprehension or watch time.
  • Cut terms that trigger many muted-keyword filters in your target audience.

For the account

  • Keep a coherent topic lane so retrieval can place the author.
  • Earn follows from people who repeatedly engage in that lane.
  • Post at a cadence where each candidate can breathe.
  • Use quotes and replies where your framing adds standalone value.
  • Optimize for low negative feedback as much as high positive feedback.
